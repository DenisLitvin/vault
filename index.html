FAQ

Why not storing seed phrase on piece of paper or iCloud directly?

Exposing your seed phrases as a plain text is risky due to common weak security habits. Hackers may access your iCloud and therefore your crypto assets. Vault, however, fortifies your seed phrases with triple-layered security: Face ID, a passcode , and an emoji. These are memorable, hard to crack, and could only reveal gibberish encrypted data to potential intruders.

How do I know you are not going to steal my crypto?

Even if we attempted, we couldn't. Vault utilizes iCloud as its backend; your data never departs your Apple account. It's entirely encrypted on your device prior to being secured in your personal iCloud account for safe storage and cross-platform accessibility.

What if you get hacked?

There is nothing to hack. We offer a tool for local seed phrase encryption, safeguarded by three security layers under your control. We don't access your Face ID, passcode, or emoji choices; they merely encrypt your seed phrases on-device. And we don't store your encrypted seed phrase. It's securely backed up to your iCloud for future retrieval.
